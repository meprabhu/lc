Synchronous Session: The Importance of Nonverbal Communication: We began the lesson by viewing The Exam, a humorous Mr. Bean video. The video is an excellent example of the power of nonverbal communication. Nonverbal communication relies heavily on facial expressions. My important takeaways are as follows: 1. The spoken word isn't the sole way to communicate. 2. Cheating is not a solution to any problem, but it may cause one in the future.

Asynchronous Session: In the third week's session, we were instructed to watch a film that guides us to perfection and explains what can derail any positive initial impression. These are the items: Wrong clothing; being late; forgetting names; continuously checking the phone; picking the wrong conversations; playing with hair; weak handshake; Avoiding making direct eye contact. Exercise in self-reflection: While conversing with a family member or close friend, pay attention to his or her verbal and nonverbal communication. Which indications do you see as positive or negative?

My reflection: During a talk with a classmate, I noted that he was quite self-assured and spoke with good pitch and tone. He wasn't speaking clearly, though, and there were too many gaps between words. Non-verbally he was creating adequate eye contact and body postures while saying things.
